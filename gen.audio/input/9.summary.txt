Lady Adelaide Winterbourne visits Holmes to report the theft of the Cat's Eye jewel from her husband's locked study. She reveals that she held the key while guests gathered nearby. The cabinet shows no signs of forced entry despite being locked securely. An anonymous letter warns of the stone before the crime occurs. Holmes agrees to investigate the impossible disappearance of the gem. Holmes inspects the study and finds a faint green fleck near the empty cabinet niche. He notices a cat hiding in a basket in the kitchen. The animal wears a torn piece of green velvet around its neck. Maid Elsie admits Miss Shaw feeds the cat cream daily. Holmes deduces the thief used the animal as a silent courier for the stolen gem. He follows the cat to find the mastermind behind the theft. Holmes and Watson follow clues from the glasshouse to a busy theater on the Strand. They discover a trapdoor leading to a dark basement chamber below. A rogue conjurer named Silas Morn hides there with accomplices. Holmes corners Morn but learns he passed the jewel to another person already. The thief planned a theatrical performance to hide the crime from plain sight. Watson and Holmes ascend to the stage to find the real culprit. Holmes spots the black cat on a trunk near a man in a red waistcoat. He identifies the man as art dealer Percival Shaw handling the stolen gem. Shaw tries to draw a hidden pistol to threaten Holmes and Watson. Holmes strikes the gun away with his cane before Shaw can fire. Watson retrieves the jewel from a small pouch on the cat's collar. The conspirators are exposed as the performance continues nearby. Holmes and Watson bring the captured thieves to Scotland Yard for arrest. He advises Lord Winterbourne on a false story to hide the true details of the case. This plan prevents the family's reputation from being ruined by gossip. Holmes ensures the guests remain silent by threatening to expose their own secrets. The Cat's Eye is placed back in its cabinet safely. Watson records the events as a lesson on observation and deduction.
