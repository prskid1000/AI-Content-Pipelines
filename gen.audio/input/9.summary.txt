President Vladimir Putin arrives in New Delhi at 6:35 PM local time. Prime Minister Narendra Modi personally greets him with a hug and handshake. The warm welcome signifies the importance of this visit. Both leaders proceed directly to the official residence on Lok Kalyan Marg. The airport arrival is part of an elaborate diplomatic protocol. The event marks Putin's first visit to India since the war in Ukraine began. The two leaders meet at Hyderabad House for the 23rd India-Russia annual summit. The agenda includes defense cooperation, energy supplies, nuclear-power collaboration, and trade. Both sides are preparing to sign multiple agreements across sectors. Key issues include labor mobility, pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, textiles, and finance. A major military logistics pact, RELOS, has been ratified by the Russian Parliament. This allows mutual use of facilities for exercises, humanitarian efforts, and disaster relief. Putin is accompanied by a large business delegation from Russia. Indian business leaders are also present in New Delhi. Economic cooperation spans pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, textiles, and labor mobility. Russian banks like Sberbank plan to increase imports from India. This aims to rebalance the long-standing trade imbalance between both nations. The discussions may result in several agreements on commerce, investment, and financial partnerships. Security has been stepped up across the capital. Sky and ground agencies remain on high alert. Movement routes have been sealed. Sensitive locations are secured with additional personnel from national and local forces. The visit is considered highly sensitive due to global tensions. A symbolic gesture includes Putin paying respects at Rajghat, Mahatma Gandhi's memorial. This underscores Russia’s intention to acknowledge shared values while renewing ties. The summit unfolds under global pressure since the Ukraine invasion began nearly four years ago. This is Putin's first visit to India during that period. Western nations closely watch this diplomatic engagement. India manages multiple strategic partnerships simultaneously. Outcomes may impact defense, energy security, and trade dynamics in South Asia. Global reactions will likely be significant across major powers. The agreements could help reshape the geopolitical balance of power. India seeks to protect its strategic autonomy amid international pressures.
